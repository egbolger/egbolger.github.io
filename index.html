Welcome! I am a PhD Student in the Computational Mathematics, Science, and Engineering (CMSE) Department at Michigan State University. I currently work in the Computational Education Research Lab (CERL) where we focus on understanding how students learn computational science and related tools, so that we can improve our teaching strategies. Outside of research, I am a teaching assistant for CMSE 381: Fundamentals of Data Science Methods. I am also passionate about outreach and mentoring young individuals interested in STEM.

Recent Activites:

  • Using Social Network Analysis techniques to analyze and model data from Slack consisting of interactions between Physics instructors looking to better incorporate computation into their courses
  • Teaching CMSE 381: Fundamentals of Data Science
  • Co-chair for the Graduate Women in Science (GWIS) Girl's Math and Science Day Committee and the Undergraduate Mentoring Committee
